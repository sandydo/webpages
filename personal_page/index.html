Short Bio

    Beside my bachelor in scientific programming I did an education as mathematical software engineer at Forschungszentrum Jülich, in the Institute of Energy and Climate Research (2006-2009). After my masters in Technomathematics (TU Berlin), I was employed at Ottobock for two years. I started my PhD research within the EU project AMYO, developing machine learning algorithms to improve the robustness of prosthetic devices. In 2014, I decided to join the machine learning group headed by Klaus-Robert Müller, where I am employed as a research scientist in the ALICE project.


Research Interests

    I am interested in "opening the black box" of machine learning algorithms, i.e. the extraction of the discriminative motifs underlying the learning machine. In the course of that, I have been working on applications in computational biology (DFG funded project on 'Learning with Sequence Data'). Beside that, I am interested in all kind of research concerning pattern recognition to control prosthetic devices as well as in deep learning, in image segmentation, and in brain-computer interfacing.
